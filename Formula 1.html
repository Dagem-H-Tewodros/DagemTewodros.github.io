Data Analysis and Visualization of Formula 1 History

Tools used:

MySQL: SQL Script

Powerbi: Dashboard

As a Formula 1 enthusiast, I embarked on a project to uncover the hidden details of the sport. Using a comprehensive dataset from Kaggle, I aimed to create an engaging Power BI dashboard that tells the story of F1 through data. Here's a look at the steps I took, the challenges faced, and the intriguing insights that emerged.

Gathering the data was the first step in this journey. I started by downloading a dataset from Kaggle, which included a variety of CSV files covering different aspects of Formula 1. These files detailed everything from the evolution of the championship seasons to race schedules, results, and standings. They also contained information about drivers, constructors, circuits, and more. The data spanned from 1950 to 2023, providing a comprehensive view of the sport's history.

Once I had the dataset, the next step was to structure it in MySQL. Importing the CSV files into MySQL allowed me to organize the data efficiently, enabling seamless querying and manipulation. This structured setup was crucial for the detailed analysis that followed.

With clean and organized data, I began to explore the intricacies of Formula 1. Analyzing championship standings revealed periods of dominance by certain drivers and constructors, such as Ferrari in the early 2000s and Mercedes in recent years. Examining circuit-specific performance highlighted tracks where specific drivers and teams consistently excelled. Comparing qualifying results with race outcomes showed drivers who frequently outperformed their starting positions, showcasing their racecraft and strategy. However, the analysis of qualifying results was limited, as the dataset only covered qualifying sessions from 2006 to 2008.

Diving deeper into pit stop data underscored the importance of timing and execution in influencing race results. The introduction of sprint qualifying sessions added a new dimension to race weekends, affecting strategies and outcomes. Each of these insights contributed to a richer understanding of the sport.

The final step was creating an interactive Power BI dashboard. This dashboard provided an app-like experience, allowing users to explore the data intuitively. It featured an interactive timeline of key events and standings, detailed profiles of drivers and constructors, visual representations of performance at different tracks, and dynamic views of race results, qualifying performance, and pit stop strategies.

This project highlighted the power of data in revealing the nuances of Formula 1. By cleaning and analyzing the data, I was able to uncover interesting stories and patterns. The Power BI dashboard made these insights accessible, offering a platform for fans to delve deeper into the sport.
